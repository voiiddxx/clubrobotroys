The Efficycyle

Efficycle is an intercollegiate design competition for undergraduate and graduate students where teams have to design and fabricate an energy efficient entirely humanle powered three-wheeled vehicle. The vehicle must be aerodynamic, highly engineered and ergonomically designed. The design should be commercially viable as a product and should be attractive to the consumers because of its visual appearance, performance, reliability and ease of operation.

Air Purifying Bicycle

Bicycles are often cited as the most efficient modes of transportation in the world. The air-purifier bike currently exists only in concept. The aim of this innovative bicycle is inhales polluted air, cleans it, and gives clean air to the cyclist and environment. It is intended to become a medium for smog free environment, generating clean air to celebrate the bicycle and making thousands of them to create an impact on the larger urban scale.

Electric Car

Electric car has often been suggested as a helpful solution to reduce oil consumption and air pollutant emissions. The aim of this electric car project is to reduce the emissions which contribute in climate change and smog, improving public health and reducing ecological damage. This Electric car has fewer moving parts than a conventional petrol/diesel car. Servicing is relatively easy, less frequent and overall cheaper than a petrol/diesel vehicle.
